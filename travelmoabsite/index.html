NATIONAL PARKS

Explore the National Parks of the Moab, Utah area.

Arches National Park

One of the main things Moab is known for is the national parks nearby. Arches National Park is a must see. Located just 5 miles north of Moab, Arches contains the world's largest concentration of natural sandstone arches. Colossal sandstone fins, massive balanced rocks, soaring pinnacles and spires dwarf visitors as they explore the park's viewpoints and hiking trails. A paved scenic drive takes visitors to many of the major viewpoints within the park. The park will delight children and adults, with many easy trails with easy access to arch exploration.

Canyonlands National Park

Canyonlands is the largest national park in Utah and is only 32 miles from Moab. There are many pullouts with spectacular views along the paved scenic drive. Hiking trails and four wheeling drive roads access backcountry areas for day or overnight trips. Take a short day hike or spend a relaxing afternoon enjoying the sunset. Whether a short or long trip, Canyonlands provides an unforgettable experience for the whole family.
